# Constraint/viol trajectory + barrier sign + metrics 4->10: starts at -d_k rising=healthy, barrier mostly-negative=healthy, early-satisfy tail=over-loose candidate

`Constraint/viol/<name>` is the TRAJECTORY of J_C - d_k over training; it starts at -d_k (random policy, J_C~0) and rises toward 0 as the policy learns to USE that constraint's resource. Reading the curve shape (not just the final value) tells you WHEN each constraint stopped being a learning signal.

DEFINITION (constraint_trpo.py:447): `viol_k = mean_cost_returns_k - d_k = J_C_k - d_k`. Sign convention: viol < 0 = satisfied (more negative = more headroom), viol = 0 = exactly at budget, viol > 0 = real violation. Mirror of margin: `Constraint/margin/<name> = adaptive_d_k - J_C ~= -viol` in the slack regime (constraint_trpo.py:449; adaptive floor `d_k^i = max(d_k, J_C + alpha*d_k)` engages only near-binding). Logged keys: `Constraint/viol/<name>`, `Constraint/margin/<name>`, `Constraint/barrier_penalty` (constraint_encoder_runner.py:289-293).

WHY viol STARTS AT -d_k AND RISES (normal dynamics, NOT a bug): at iter 0 the policy is random and pays ~0 cost, so J_C~0 and viol = 0 - d_k = -d_k (its most-negative point). As the policy chases reward and starts spending that resource, J_C climbs and viol rises toward 0. So "viol rising from a big negative toward a small negative" = the policy LEARNING to use the budget = healthy. This is the opposite of alarming. Verified against baseline trpo_baseline_260608_172710 (user plot read 2026-06-14): thruster_util viol starts ~-40 (= -d_k, d_k=40) and converges ~-7.7 (J_C/d_k=0.807); rp_vel_settling converges ~-10 (d_k=20, J_C/d_k=0.504). The convergence value of viol = -d_k*(1 - J_C/d_k).

BARRIER_PENALTY SIGN (mostly-negative = healthy): `Constraint/barrier_penalty = -(1/t) * sum_k log(margin_k)` (constraint_trpo.py:464, t=barrier_t=100). Because margin sits at 1-40 in the slack regime, log(margin)>0, so barrier is NEGATIVE in normal operation = the barrier is NOT interfering with reward (all 10 constraints deep inside budget). A POSITIVE spike means some constraint's margin dropped below 1 (approaching its limit) and the barrier is braking hard. So "mostly negative + a few positive peaks" (baseline: late=-0.13, 3 spikes>0.1 max 0.71; state_std: last=-0.119, spikes=0) is the healthy pattern, NOT a problem. CAVEAT linking to the looseness question: barrier ~always negative ALSO means "constraints barely shape learning" -- health (policy obeys) and looseness (no pressure) are two faces of the same curve. Only thruster_util peaks positive, i.e. is the only constraint giving real pressure.

THE "EARLY-SATISFY TAIL" OBSERVATION (user, 2026-06-14, qualitative -- quantitative confirm needs exp-analyze viol time-series): the 8 non-binding constraints converge at J_C/d_k = 0.0-0.4, meaning their viol settles near -d_k very early and then carries a dead signal for the rest of training. Only thruster_util (0.807) and rp_vel_settling (0.504) stay live pressures. A constraint whose viol drops back DOWN toward -d_k after an early bump = the policy learned to fully avoid that cost early = "over-loose / satisfied-too-early" candidate. This is the data backing the user's intuition that the constraint set may be partly placeholder. CAVEAT: early-satisfy alone does NOT mean "delete it" -- a deep-slack constraint can be (A) genuinely over-loose, (B) auto-satisfied by the task (attitude: tracking reward keeps |att| at 1% of the 80deg limit), or (C) a dormant safety guard that only wakes under fault/perturbation (joint1_pos, cumul_yaw -- essential headroom for fault-tolerant control). Classifying (A)/(B)/(C) per constraint requires the viol time-series + threshold-touch rate, NOT the final J_C/d_k alone. The (1)truly-loose vs (2)binding-but-satisfied split is decided by per-constraint barrier-penalty contribution, not the viol final value.
